What is MCDM Weighted Scoring?

The Weighted Scoring Model — also known as Simple Additive Weighting (SAW) in MCDM literature — is the most widely used multi-criteria decision analysis technique in engineering and business. It evaluates multiple alternatives across multiple criteria simultaneously, combining quantitative and qualitative factors into a single comparable score.

How the Calculation Works

Weighted Score = ∑ (Normalised Weighti × Scorei)

  • Criterion weights — Reflect relative importance. Normalised so they sum to 1.
  • Raw scores (1–10) — Rate each alternative per criterion.
  • Direction — For Cost criteria (lower = better), scores are automatically inverted.

Common Applications

ApplicationTypical CriteriaAlternatives
Supplier SelectionPrice, quality, delivery, reliabilitySupplier A, B, C...
Machine PurchaseCost, speed, precision, maintenanceMachine options
Project PrioritisationROI, feasibility, strategic fit, riskProject proposals
Location SelectionCost, logistics, workforce, infrastructureSite options

Benefit vs Cost Criteria

  • Benefit (Higher = Better): Quality, speed, reliability — score 10 is best
  • Cost (Lower = Better): Price, lead time, defect rate — score 1 is best, automatically inverted

Tips for Better Decisions

  • Involve multiple stakeholders when assigning weights — reveals disagreements early
  • Define what a score of 10 means for each criterion before scoring
  • Perform sensitivity analysis — change the top weight ±20% and check if ranking changes
  • SAW is one of 10+ validated MCDM methods alongside MARCOS, COPRAS, VIKOR, EDAS, and WASPAS
