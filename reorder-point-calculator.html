What is the Reorder Point?

The Reorder Point (ROP) is the inventory level at which a new replenishment order should be placed. When stock falls to this level, you have just enough inventory to cover demand during the supplier's lead time — plus a safety buffer to handle variability. Getting the ROP right prevents both stockouts and excess inventory.

The Reorder Point Formula

ROP = (Average Daily Demand × Lead Time) + Safety Stock

The first term covers expected demand during the lead time. Safety stock covers the unexpected — demand spikes or delivery delays. Without safety stock, any deviation from average means a stockout.

Service Level and Z-Factor

The service level determines how much safety stock to hold. A 95% service level means a 5% chance of stockout per replenishment cycle. The Z-factor translates this probability into a safety stock multiplier:

  • 90% service level → Z = 1.28
  • 95% service level → Z = 1.65
  • 99% service level → Z = 2.33
  • 99.9% service level → Z = 3.09

Higher service levels require significantly more safety stock. Moving from 95% to 99% increases safety stock by roughly 40% — a cost-benefit trade-off every supply chain manager faces.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using scheduled lead time instead of actual lead time — Always measure real historical delivery times
  • Ignoring lead time variability — A supplier with average 5-day lead time but ±3 days variance needs more safety stock than one with ±0.5 days
  • Setting ROP once and never reviewing — Demand patterns change seasonally; review your ROP at least quarterly
  • Using the same service level for all SKUs — Critical components deserve 99%+; low-value, easily replaceable items may need only 90%
