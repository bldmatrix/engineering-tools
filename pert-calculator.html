What is PERT?

PERT (Program Evaluation and Review Technique) is a project management method developed by the US Navy in 1957 for the Polaris submarine missile programme. It uses three time estimates per task to produce a statistically weighted expected duration — directly accounting for uncertainty in project planning.

The Three PERT Estimates

  • O — Optimistic: Minimum time if everything goes perfectly. Roughly 1% probability of completing faster.
  • M — Most Likely: Most realistic duration under normal working conditions.
  • P — Pessimistic: Maximum time if everything goes wrong. Roughly 1% probability of taking longer.

PERT Formulas

Expected Duration: tE = (O + 4M + P) ÷ 6

Standard Deviation: σ = (P − O) ÷ 6

Variance: σ² = [(P − O) ÷ 6]²

For a full project path, total tE = sum of all task tE values. Total σ = square root of the sum of all variances.

How to Use the Multi-Task Planner

  • Add all tasks on your critical path
  • The 95% completion date (tE + 2σ) is a reliable deadline to commit to stakeholders
  • Tasks with high σ relative to tE carry the most scheduling risk — investigate those first
  • Use the single task estimator to quickly check individual activities before adding them to the planner
