What are Cp and Cpk?

Cp and Cpk are process capability indices used in Statistical Process Control (SPC) to measure how well a manufacturing process produces output within specification limits. Together they answer two different questions about your process.

Cp vs Cpk — What's the Difference?

  • Cp (Process Capability) — Measures the potential capability assuming the process is perfectly centred between the spec limits. It only looks at process spread relative to the tolerance.
  • Cpk (Process Capability Index) — Measures actual capability accounting for where the process mean sits within the spec limits. Always equal to or less than Cp.

If Cp is high but Cpk is low, your process has enough spread to be capable but is not centred correctly — it is shifted toward one of the spec limits.

How to Improve Cpk

  • Low Cp AND low Cpk: Process variation is too wide. Reduce variability through machine calibration, operator training, or material consistency.
  • High Cp but low Cpk: Process is capable but off-centre. Adjust the process mean toward the target — shift the distribution without changing its spread.
  • Both high: Process is capable and centred. Implement control charts to maintain this state.
